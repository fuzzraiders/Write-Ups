# 🤝 Team Collaboration

---

## 📖 About

The **Team Collaboration** section focuses on **joint technical execution**, where multiple FuzzRaiders members work together on the same target (CTF, lab, or machine) and produce a unified write-up.

This repository captures how the team collaborates in real-world scenarios, combining different skills to analyze, exploit, and document complex challenges.

Content emphasizes **team-based problem solving, technical depth, and coordinated execution**, ensuring each write-up reflects collective effort.

---

## 🎯 Purpose

This section exists to:

* Document collaborative CTF and lab engagements
* Showcase team-based exploitation workflows
* Combine multiple skill sets into one structured write-up
* Simulate real-world red team collaboration
* Build coordination between different roles and domains

The focus is on **how the team works together technically**, not individual performance.

## 📚 Content Scope

Write-ups in this section may include:

* CTF challenges solved collaboratively
* Lab environments worked on by multiple members
* Full machine compromises (end-to-end attack paths)
* Multi-stage exploitation scenarios
* Cross-domain collaboration (Web + AD + PrivEsc, etc.)

All content reflects **team execution and coordination**, not solo work.

---

## 🧪 Write-Up Standards

Each write-up must follow the **FuzzRaiders Collaboration Standard**:

* One unified and structured write-up (not split per person)
* Clear attack flow from start to finish
* Step-by-step reproducibility
* Proper use of tools and commands
* Relevant screenshots where necessary

---

## 👥 Team Contribution Model

Each write-up must clearly show **who contributed and how**:

* 👤 Member 1 – Recon / Enumeration
* 👤 Member 2 – Initial Exploitation
* 👤 Member 3 – Privilege Escalation
* 👤 Member 4 – Post-Exploitation
* 👤 Member 5+ – Support / Analysis / Validation

👉 Even if **4–6 members** work on one target, the output must be **ONE clean professional write-up**.

---

## 🧠 FuzzRaiders Standard (Core Principle)

A collaboration write-up must:

* Reflect coordinated team effort
* Show logical attack progression
* Combine multiple perspectives into one solution
* Demonstrate real-world teamwork in offensive security
